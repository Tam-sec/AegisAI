

# create a police for the new empoyment

Below is a simple **UK-style** New Employment and Induction Policy you can use for new employees. In the UK, employers must provide the main written statement of employment particulars on or before the employee’s first day of work, and accurate starter information helps ensure correct pay, tax, and National Insurance from the start.[^1](https://www.acas.org.uk/what-must-be-written-in-an-employment-contract/what-the-written-statement-must-include)[^3](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/simplifying-the-onboarding-process-for-new-starters-communication-resources)

## Policy use

This draft is suitable as a new starter, onboarding, or commencement of employment policy for a small business or handbook section. ACAS also provides written statement templates and says some employment terms can be given separately, but no later than 2 months after the start date.[^4](https://www.acas.org.uk/what-must-be-written-in-an-employment-contract/when-an-employee-must-get-their-written-statement)

## Policy draft

### New Employment and Induction Policy

**1. Purpose**
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all new employees are appointed, onboarded, and inducted in a consistent, fair, and lawful manner. It also sets out the responsibilities of the Company and the employee during the early stages of employment.

**2. Scope**
This policy applies to all new employees joining **\[Company Name]**, including full-time, part-time, fixed-term, and probationary staff. Where relevant, parts of this policy may also be applied to workers, agency staff, or temporary personnel.

**3. Policy statement**
\[Company Name] is committed to making sure every new employee receives the information, support, and documentation needed to start work effectively. The Company will provide the required employment documentation, complete onboarding checks, and deliver a suitable induction into the employee’s role, team, and workplace expectations.

**4. Employment documentation**
Before or on the first day of employment, the employee will be given the main written terms of employment, including job title, start date, place of work, pay, and working hours. Any additional terms not included in the main statement will be provided within the required timeframe.
The employee must provide accurate personal, payroll, tax, and right-to-work information when requested. Failure to provide correct information may delay payroll, benefits, access, or completion of onboarding checks.

**5. Pre-employment checks**
Employment will be subject to satisfactory pre-employment checks where applicable. These may include proof of identity, right to work, references, qualification checks, criminal record checks where the role requires them, and any other lawful screening relevant to the position.
Any offer of employment may be withdrawn or employment may be reviewed if false, misleading, or incomplete information is provided.

**6. Induction**
All new employees will complete an induction process appropriate to their role and level of responsibility. Induction may include:

* Introduction to the business, team, role, and reporting lines.
* Review of key policies and procedures.
* Health and safety information, emergency procedures, and workplace rules.
* IT, security, equipment, and data protection requirements.
* Training necessary to perform the role safely and effectively.

**7. Probationary period**
New employees will normally be subject to a probationary period of **\[insert length, e.g. 3 or 6 months]**. During probation, performance, conduct, attendance, and suitability for the role will be reviewed.
The Company may confirm appointment, extend probation where appropriate, or terminate employment in line with the contract and applicable procedures.

**8. Employee responsibilities**
Employees are expected to:

* Provide accurate and complete onboarding information.
* Attend induction sessions and mandatory training.
* Read and comply with workplace policies and procedures.
* Raise any questions or concerns promptly with their manager or HR.
* Maintain appropriate standards of conduct, attendance, and performance from the start of employment.

**9. Manager responsibilities**
Managers are responsible for ensuring that:

* New starters are prepared for before their first day.
* Required documentation and checks are completed.
* The employee receives an induction plan and necessary training.
* Role expectations are explained clearly.
* Probation reviews are carried out on time and properly recorded.

**10. Payroll and starter information**
New employees must provide all information requested for payroll and tax purposes as soon as possible. The Company will process starter information in line with HMRC requirements so employees can be paid correctly and placed on the correct tax and National Insurance records.

**11. Related policies**
This policy should be read alongside the employee’s contract or written statement, probation policy, disciplinary policy, grievance policy, health and safety policy, data protection policy, and any relevant employee handbook provisions.

**12. Review**
This policy will be reviewed periodically and may be amended to reflect legal, operational, or organisational changes.

## Legal points

ACAS says the principal statement must include key terms such as employer and employee names, start date, job title, workplace, pay, and working hours.[^2](https://www.acas.org.uk/what-must-be-written-in-an-employment-contract/what-the-written-statement-must-include)
HMRC’s onboarding guidance says employers should understand what starter information to provide and submit it in the correct format so new employees are paid correctly.[^3](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/simplifying-the-onboarding-process-for-new-starters-communication-resources)
